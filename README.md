# churn-analysis-powerbi
Customer churn analysis using Power BI

## Overview
This project analyzes bank customer data to identify key factors that influence customer churn.
The goal is to provide insights that can help improve customer retention and business decision-making.

## Dataset
The dataset contains approximately 10,000 customers with the followinf features:
-Age
-Gender
-Credit Score
-Account Balance
-Country
-Activity Status
-Credit Card Ownership

# Tools Used
-Power BI

## Key Insights
-Overall churn
"One of the first things I noticed is that the overall churn rate is around 20%, which is quite high, This indicates a potential issue with customer retention."

-Customer Activity
"I found that Inactive customers are much more likely to churn, This shows that customers engagement plays a critical role in retention."

-Age Groups
"Customers between 50 and 60 years old have the highest churn rate , this suggests that older customers may not be fully satisfied with the bank's services."

-Credit Card Ownership
"Customers who do not own a credit card tend to churn more, This indicates that customers who use more banking products are more likely to stay."

-Credit Score
"Customers with low credit scores, especially below 400, have significantly higher churn rates, This shows a strong relationship between financial risk and customer retention."

-Country Analysis
"The data also shows differences across countries, some regions have higher churn rates, which suggests that location-based strategies may be needed."

-Account Balance
"Customers with lower account balances are more likely to leave, This could indicate low engagement or low perceived value."

## Recommendation
-Increase engagement for Inactive customers
-Encourage customers to adopt more products (e.g., credit cards)
-Focus on high-risk groups (low credit score, older customers)
-Develop targeted strategies based on region
